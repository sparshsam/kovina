# Open Product Family — Hierarchy

## Three-Level Brand Architecture

```
Level 1    KOVINA          Parent ecosystem
           Inter Black 900
           -0.03em tracking
           Uppercase
           Frozen — never modify

Level 2    OPEN            Product family
           Inter Bold 700
           0.05em tracking
           Uppercase
           Reusable — shared by all Open apps

Level 3    Reader          Individual product
           Inter Regular 400
           0em tracking
           Title case
           Unique per application
```

## Why Three Levels?

**Level 1 — KOVINA** is the philosophical and visual foundation. It represents the entire ecosystem. It never changes.

**Level 2 — OPEN** is the bridge between the parent brand and individual applications. It signals that an application follows Kovina standards, uses Kovina infrastructure, and belongs to the Kovina ecosystem. This is what makes a collection of apps feel like a family.

**Level 3 — Product** is the application's own identity. It is unique, has its own accent color, its own icon, its own roadmap. The product name is what users actually refer to ("I use OpenReader") — but the OPEN prefix ties it back to the family.

## Visual Weight

In any composition, the weights should read clearly:

| Element | Weight | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| KOVINA | Black (900) | Dominant — ecosystem anchor |
| OPEN | Bold (700) | Supporting — family identifier |
| Product name | Regular (400) | Subordinate — individual identity |
